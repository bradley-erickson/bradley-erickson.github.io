<b>Abstract:</b> Stanzas  of  lyrics  typically  have  rhyming  patterns associated  with  them.   For  example,  if  there  is  a stanza that contains four verses where the first and third share a similar rhyme scheme and the second and fourth share a similar rhyme scheme, then the verses follow an ABAB pattern.  These patterns are often determined strictly by hand.  In this paper,we attempt to create automated methods for deter-mining these patterns. We approach this process bylooking at the phonetics within any given verse and comparing them to those of the other verses.  The three methods we came up with are comparing the rhyming phonetics of the last words,  the rhyming phonetics of all the words, and all the phonetics of all words between verses.  We apply these methods to poetics lyrics that have predetermined rhyming schemes to see how our methods compare.
